How to Make Google OAuth Universal (Publishing Your App)

Currently, your Google OAuth app is in "Testing" mode, meaning only manually added "Test Users" can log in. To allow ANY Gmail user to log in automatically, you must push the app to "In Production".

⚠️ CRITICAL WARNING FOR GMAIL APPS: 
The `gmail.send` scope you are using is classified by Google as a "Restricted Scope". Publishing an app with restricted scopes for the general public requires a formal Cloud Application Security Assessment (CASA) by a Google-approved third-party auditor, which can be a lengthy and expensive process. 

If you are only using this internally or with a limited number of known clients (up to 100 users), IT IS HIGHLY RECOMMENDED to keep the app in "Testing" mode and just add their emails manually to bypass the audit requirement.

If you still wish to make it public for anyone to sign up, here are the exact steps:

STEP 1: PREPARE YOUR ASSETS
1. You must have a live, public website with a homepage.
2. You must have a public Privacy Policy URL (detailing how you use Google user data).
3. You must have a public Terms of Service URL.

STEP 2: VERIFY YOUR DOMAIN
1. Go to Google Search Console (search.google.com/search-console).
2. Add your production domain (e.g., https://yourdomain.com) and verify ownership (usually by adding a DNS TXT record).
3. Back in Google Cloud Console -> APIs & Services -> Domain Verification, add your verified domain.

STEP 3: UPDATE OAUTH BRANDING
1. In Google Cloud Console, go to "Branding".
2. Add your homepage URL, Privacy Policy URL, and Terms of Service URL.
3. Add your Authorized Domain (the one you just verified).

STEP 4: PUBLISH THE APP
1. Go to "Audience" in the sidebar.
2. Scroll to the bottom and click the "Publish App" button.
3. Because you are using a restricted scope (`gmail.send`), Google will immediately prompt you to submit your app for verification.

STEP 5: THE VERIFICATION PROCESS
1. You will need to submit a justification explaining why you need Gmail access.
2. You will need to provide a YouTube video demonstrating exactly how a user logs in and how your app sends an email on their behalf.
3. Follow Google's instructions to undergo the third-party security assessment.

Once approved by Google, the "This app isn't verified" warning screen will completely disappear, and anyone in the world with a Google account can connect their email instantly via the "Sign in with Google" button.
